Design Principles

Andrew J. Cornelius

Rule of Thirds

Shutterstock

Shutterstock uses the principle of the Rule of Thirds by placing its icons and buttons in the center and on the edges of the screen. Stutterstock also has each section devided up into sections that align with the vertical and horizontal lines that make up the Rule of Thirds.

White Space and Clean Design

Pinterest

Pinterest uses the design principle of white space and clean design very well by evenly and consistently spacing out its images and pages. Even with images that are different sizes they all are spaced in a specific way and as you scroll through it each alternating image catches your eye.

Hick's Law

Amazon

Amazon is a perfect example of Hick's Law as it has so many different items to choose from as well as buttons that can show you even more items. You can even search for specific items and all of this keeps you on their sight and increases the chance that you will buy something from them.
